What we do

Sandra and Edward Meyer Cancer Center

Our mission is to ensure that patients have access to emerging new therapies in a supportive and caring environment, while training future researchers and recruiting leaders in cancer research and clinical care.

Care

Personalized, compassionate care

We provide care that is state-of-the-art, not out-of-the-box. We partner with each patient to devise the best available evaluation and treatment program — standard care or an innovative clinical trial. A full range of resources is available: nutrition, psychosocial support, complementary medicine, rehabilitation, pain management, and specialists across all medical fields.

Discover

Translating science into cures

By delving into basic biological processes, genetic abnormalities, and pathological states, we make unparalleled progress in understanding how cancer develops — and translate that knowledge into new approaches for prevention, treatment, and cure. Our two basic-science and two translational/clinical research programs are powered by a centralized biobank built with the Englander Institute for Precision Medicine.

Teach

Training the next generation

Founded in 1898 and affiliated with NewYork-Presbyterian Hospital since 1927, Weill Cornell Medicine ranks among the country's top medical research centers. Beyond MD programs, the Weill Cornell Graduate School of Medical Sciences offers Ph.D. degrees and a joint MD-Ph.D. with the neighboring Sloan-Kettering Institute and The Rockefeller University. Graduate students, postdocs and clinical fellows are valued members of every research lab.
